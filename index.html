About Me


Until a month ago, I never considered attending a coding academy. My fields of interest were education and the social sciences. In particular, I liked learning how social science methods, specifically statistical techniques, can be used in educational research. Using SPSS and Excel introduced me to syntax and formulas, seemingly within the family of computer coding, that speak to the software to run the analyses, but I had a very surface level understanding of this language.

I currently work as a policy and research analyst at the Independent Colleges of Indiana (ICI). My colleagues coordinated a coding academy, Smart Launch Tech, alongside Eleven Fifty for Indiana's private college students. A couple weeks prior to launch, ICI's new president, Dr. David Wantz, asked if I would attend to develop my technical skills, data software knowledge, and insight into this important academic area for future ICI educational programming.

I arrived and realized that a basic coding understanding allows for a more confident posture toward technical communication and problem solving. My college-aged classmates and I have a beginner’s understanding of code and left with concrete projects that can be built upon to improve our skills. They are now better positioned to meet Indianapolis's technical businesses’ demand for workers with coding and social skills. For my professional work, my exposure to coding allows for more complex and efficient SPSS, Statistica, and Excel analyses. This academy is a beginning point for students, but one that provides the support and skills needed to confidently continue self-learning directed toward a successful tech career.
